I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings

by Maya Angelou


  1. A free bird leaps on the back

    2. Of the wind and floats downstream

    Till the current ends and dips his wing

    In the orange suns rays

    And dares to claim the sky.


  2. But a BIRD that stalks down his narrow cage

    3. Can seldom see through his bars of rage

    His wings are clipped and his feet are tied

    So he opens his throat to sing.


  3. The caged bird sings with a fearful trill

    4. Of things unknown but longed for still

    And his tune is heard on the distant hill for

    The caged birdsings of freedom.


  4. The free bird thinks of another breeze

    5. And the trade winds soft through

    The sighing trees

    And the fat worms waiting on a dawn-bright

    Lawn and he names the sky his own


  5. But a caged BIRD stands on the grave of dreams

    6. His shadow shouts on a nightmare scream

    His wings are clipped and his feet are tied

    So he opens his throat to sing.


  6. The caged bird sings with

    7. A fearful trill of things unknown

    But longed for still and his

    Tune is heard on the distant hill

    For the caged bird sings of freedom.
